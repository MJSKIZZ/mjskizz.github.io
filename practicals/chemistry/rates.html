Effect of concentration on rate (disappearing cross)

Sodium thiosulfate reacts with hydrochloric acid to produce a fine sulfur precipitate, which turns the solution cloudy.

Simulation

0.20 mol/dm³
Elapsed
0.0 s
Rate (1/time)
— s⁻¹

Equation

Na₂S₂O₃(aq) + 2HCl(aq) → 2NaCl(aq) + SO₂(g) + S(s) + H₂O(l)

Method

  1. Measure 50 cm³ of sodium thiosulfate (different concentrations) into a conical flask.
  2. Place the flask on a piece of paper with a black cross drawn on it.
  3. Add 10 cm³ of dilute hydrochloric acid, start a timer and swirl once.
  4. Stop the timer when the cross is no longer visible through the flask from directly above.
  5. Repeat with different Na₂S₂O₃ concentrations (dilute with water, keep total volume constant at 50 cm³).
  6. Calculate rate = 1 / time and plot rate against concentration.

Variables

Independent: Concentration of Na₂S₂O₃
Dependent: Time for the cross to disappear
Control: Volume of HCl, temperature, depth of solution, same cross & observer.
