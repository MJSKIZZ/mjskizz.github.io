Paper chromatography

Separate a mixture of food dyes using paper chromatography. Calculate Rf = distance travelled by spot / distance travelled by solvent.

Method

  1. Draw a pencil line 2 cm from the bottom of a piece of chromatography paper.
  2. Put small spots of each dye on the line (A, B, C, D).
  3. Place the paper in a beaker with a small amount of solvent (water). The solvent must be below the pencil line.
  4. Cover with a lid so solvent doesn't evaporate.
  5. Let the solvent rise up the paper. Remove before it reaches the top.
  6. Mark the solvent front with pencil. Measure the distance travelled by the solvent and each spot.
  7. Rf = distance travelled by spot ÷ distance travelled by solvent. Rf is between 0 and 1.

Variables

Independent: Identity of the dye / mixture
Dependent: Distance travelled by the spot (and therefore Rf)
Control: Same solvent, same paper, same run time, pencil line (pencil doesn't dissolve).
