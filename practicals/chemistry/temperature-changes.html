Energy change in neutralisation

Investigate the effect of varying the volume of NaOH on the temperature change when reacted with a fixed volume of HCl.

Simulation

10

— °C

Method

  1. Use a measuring cylinder to put 30 cm³ of HCl into a polystyrene cup (stood in a beaker for insulation).
  2. Measure the starting temperature of the HCl with a thermometer.
  3. Measure 5 cm³ of NaOH and add it to the cup. Stir with the thermometer.
  4. Record the maximum temperature reached.
  5. Repeat adding in 5 cm³ portions of NaOH (total 10, 15, 20, 25, 30, 35, 40 cm³) and record the max temperature each time (or repeat in fresh cups).
  6. Plot temperature rise against NaOH volume.

Variables

Independent: Volume of NaOH (cm³)
Dependent: Maximum temperature of the mixture (°C)
Control: Volume & concentration of HCl, same concentrations of NaOH, same cup/thermometer.
