Titration — acid and alkali

Find the concentration of a dilute acid by titration against a standard alkali using an indicator.

Method

  1. Use a pipette to accurately measure 25.0 cm³ of sodium hydroxide (alkali) into a conical flask.
  2. Add a few drops of phenolphthalein indicator (pink in alkali, colourless in acid).
  3. Fill a burette with dilute hydrochloric acid of known concentration. Record the initial reading at the bottom of the meniscus.
  4. Add acid slowly from the burette, swirling the flask.
  5. As the end point approaches, add drop by drop until the pink just disappears.
  6. Record the final burette reading. Titre = final − initial.
  7. Repeat until you get concordant titres (within 0.10 cm³).

Variables

Independent: Volume of acid added (cm³)
Dependent: pH / indicator colour
Control: Volume & concentration of alkali, same indicator, same pipette/burette.

Calculation

HCl + NaOH → NaCl + H₂O. Moles NaOH = C × V. Since 1:1, moles HCl = moles NaOH. C(HCl) = moles / V(HCl).
