Electrolysis of aqueous solutions

Electrolyse aqueous solutions using inert carbon electrodes. Identify and test the products at each electrode.

Method

  1. Half-fill a small beaker with the solution.
  2. Put two inert graphite electrodes in the solution, without touching, connected via a low-voltage d.c. power supply.
  3. For gas products, cover each electrode with an inverted test tube to collect any gas.
  4. Switch on the power and leave for a few minutes.
  5. Test any gas collected: pop test for hydrogen, glowing splint for oxygen, damp blue litmus bleaches for chlorine.
  6. Inspect the cathode for any metal deposited and note any colour change of the solution.

Rules

Cathode (reduction): Positive metal ion is discharged if it is less reactive than hydrogen. Otherwise hydrogen gas is released.
Anode (oxidation): If a halide (Cl⁻, Br⁻, I⁻) is present, the halogen is discharged. Otherwise oxygen gas is released from hydroxide ions.
