Water analysis & purification

Method — Analysis

  1. Test each sample with universal indicator and compare the colour to a pH chart.
  2. Weigh an evaporating basin. Add 10 cm³ of the sample and evaporate to dryness over a water bath.
  3. Re-weigh the basin. The increase in mass = mass of dissolved solids.
  4. Lower dissolved solids + neutral pH ≈ purer water.

Method — Simple distillation

  1. Heat the water sample in a flask with a delivery tube.
  2. Water boils at 100 °C — the vapour is pure. Dissolved solids (e.g. salt) stay in the flask.
  3. Condense the vapour in a Liebig condenser. Collect the condensate in a clean beaker.
  4. Test the collected water — low dissolved solids, pH ≈ 7.

Variables

Independent: Water sample / purification step.
Dependent: pH and mass of dissolved solids.
Control: Same sample volume, same evaporating basin, same indicator.
