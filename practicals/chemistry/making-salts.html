Preparing copper sulfate crystals

Prepare a pure, dry sample of hydrated copper(II) sulfate (CuSO₄·5H₂O) from copper(II) oxide (an insoluble base) and sulfuric acid.

Ready
Observation
—

Equation

CuO(s) + H₂SO₄(aq) → CuSO₄(aq) + H₂O(l)

Method

  1. Measure 25 cm³ of dilute sulfuric acid into a beaker and warm gently.
  2. Add a spatula of copper(II) oxide and stir. Keep adding in excess (the solid at the bottom will not all react) so you know all the acid is used up.
  3. Filter the mixture to remove the unreacted CuO, leaving a clear blue copper sulfate solution.
  4. Pour the filtrate into an evaporating basin and heat over a water bath to evaporate some water (until a hot-saturated solution forms).
  5. Leave the solution to cool and crystallise. Filter and pat the crystals dry between filter paper.

Variables / Considerations

Independent: The reactants used (acid + insoluble base).
Dependent: Mass of pure salt produced.
Control: Same concentration of acid; CuO added in excess; slow crystallisation for large pure crystals.
