Human reaction time — ruler drop

Plan and carry out an investigation into a factor affecting human reaction time. Uses s = ½gt² to convert distance caught to time.

Method

  1. Subject sits with their arm resting on a table, hand over the edge, thumb and finger apart.
  2. Hold a 30 cm ruler so that the 0 cm mark is level with the top of their thumb.
  3. Without warning, release the ruler. The subject catches it as quickly as possible.
  4. Record the distance on the ruler at the point where it was caught.
  5. Use s = ½gt² → t = √(2s/g) to convert distance to reaction time (g = 9.81 m/s²).
  6. Repeat at least 3 times per condition and calculate the mean.
  7. Compare between conditions (e.g. before/after caffeine, tired, different ages).

Variables

Independent: Condition (e.g. caffeine dose, practice, tiredness)
Dependent: Reaction time (calculated from distance)
Control: Same subject's posture, same ruler, same tester, no warning.
