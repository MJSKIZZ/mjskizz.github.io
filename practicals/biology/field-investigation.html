Field investigation — quadrats & transects

Measure the population size of daisies in a field using random sampling with a quadrat.

Method — random sampling

  1. Lay two long tape measures at right angles along two edges of the sample area.
  2. Use a random number generator to choose coordinates and place a 0.5 m × 0.5 m quadrat.
  3. Count the number of the species (e.g. daisies) inside the quadrat.
  4. Repeat for at least 10 samples to get a reliable mean.
  5. Mean per m² = mean per quadrat ÷ area of quadrat.
  6. Estimate total population: mean per m² × total area of field.

Method — transect (belt)

  1. Stretch a line across a gradient (e.g. sun → shade).
  2. Place a quadrat at regular intervals along the line.
  3. Count the species in each quadrat.
  4. Plot distribution vs distance to see how abiotic factors affect populations.

Variables

Independent: Position of the quadrat (sampling)
Dependent: Number of individuals of the species
Control: Quadrat size, same species identification, same time of year.
