Effect of light intensity on photosynthesis

Count the oxygen bubbles released by pondweed (elodea/cabomba) as a measure of the rate of photosynthesis. Apply the inverse square law.

Method

  1. Fill a beaker with water and a small amount of sodium hydrogencarbonate (source of CO₂).
  2. Cut a piece of pondweed ~5 cm, place it upside-down in the beaker with the cut end up.
  3. Position a lamp a measured distance from the beaker.
  4. Leave for 5 minutes to acclimatise.
  5. Count the number of oxygen bubbles released in 1 minute (or collect gas in a measuring cylinder).
  6. Repeat at different distances (10, 20, 30, 40, 50 cm). Do three repeats and take a mean.
  7. Plot bubble count (rate) against 1/d² (proportional to light intensity).

Variables

Independent: Distance from lamp (light intensity = 1/d²)
Dependent: Rate of bubble production
Control: Temperature (use a heat shield/water bath), CO₂ concentration, same piece of pondweed.
