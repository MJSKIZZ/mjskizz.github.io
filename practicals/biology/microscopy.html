Microscopy — plant & animal cells

Use a light microscope to observe and draw plant and animal cells. Calculate magnification.

Simulation

50
Eyepiece
×10
Objective
×10
Total magnification
×100
Field of view
1.8 mm

Method

  1. Place a prepared slide on the stage and clip it into place.
  2. Start with the lowest-power objective (×4) and use the coarse focus to move the stage close to the lens.
  3. Look through the eyepiece and slowly turn the coarse focus away from the slide until cells come into view.
  4. Use the fine focus to sharpen the image, then switch to a higher objective (×10, then ×40).
  5. Draw a labelled diagram. Include a scale bar and the total magnification used.

Variables

Independent: Objective lens (magnification)
Dependent: Amount of detail visible (field of view)
Control: Same specimen, same stain, same microscope.

Magnification formula

total magnification = eyepiece × objective

real size = image size ÷ magnification
