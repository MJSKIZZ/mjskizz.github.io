Food tests

Use qualitative reagents to test for the presence of starch, sugars, proteins and lipids.

Method

  1. Prepare a food sample by grinding in water if solid.
  2. Starch — iodine test: add a few drops of iodine. Blue-black = starch.
  3. Reducing sugars — Benedict's: add Benedict's reagent and heat in a water bath for a few minutes. Brick-red precipitate = reducing sugar.
  4. Protein — biuret: add biuret reagent. Purple/violet = protein.
  5. Lipids — ethanol emulsion: shake with ethanol, then pour into water. Cloudy white emulsion = lipid.
  6. Always include a control (distilled water) for comparison.

Variables

Independent: Food sample and chosen reagent
Dependent: Colour change observed
Control: Same reagent volumes, same temperature (where heated), same observation time.

Safety

Wear goggles. Ethanol is flammable — keep away from flames, use a water bath to heat Benedict's, not a direct flame.
