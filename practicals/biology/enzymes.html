Effect of pH on amylase activity

Measure time taken for amylase to completely break down starch using iodine as an indicator. Optimum pH for amylase ≈ 7.

Method

  1. Place a drop of iodine into each well of a spotting tile.
  2. In a test tube, mix 2 cm³ of starch solution with 1 cm³ of amylase solution and 1 cm³ of buffer at a chosen pH.
  3. Start a stopwatch.
  4. Every 30 seconds take a drop of the mixture and add it to a well with iodine.
  5. Iodine turns blue-black if starch is still present, and stays orange once the starch has been broken down.
  6. Record the time taken for the iodine to no longer change colour.
  7. Repeat for buffers of pH 2, 4, 6, 7, 8, 10, 12. Plot rate (1/time) against pH.

Variables

Independent: pH of buffer
Dependent: Time for starch to be fully broken down
Control: Concentration & volume of amylase, starch, temperature, same drop size.
