Effect of force on acceleration (F = ma)

Use a trolley on a track connected over a pulley to a falling mass. Measure acceleration at different forces.

Simulation

100 g

Total (accelerated) mass = trolley + all 500 g of masses (keep them on the trolley; just move them to the hanger to change the force).

Acceleration / m/s²

—

Method

  1. Set up a trolley on a horizontal runway with a pulley at the end. Connect it via a string to a mass hanger.
  2. Record the total mass being accelerated (trolley + all masses, including those on the hanger).
  3. Use two light gates or a ticker-timer to measure the acceleration of the trolley.
  4. Start with 100 g on the hanger. The force is weight of the hanger = mg.
  5. Release the trolley and record acceleration.
  6. Transfer 100 g from the trolley to the hanger (total mass stays the same). Repeat.
  7. Plot a graph of acceleration (y) against force (x). Gradient = 1/mass.

Variables

Independent: Weight on hanger (force) / N
Dependent: Acceleration / m s⁻²
Control: Total mass accelerated, same track, friction-compensated, straight line of pull.
