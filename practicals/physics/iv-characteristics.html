I–V characteristics

Investigate the I–V characteristics of a fixed resistor, a filament lamp and a diode.

Method

  1. Set up a circuit with a PSU (d.c., variable voltage), an ammeter in series, a voltmeter in parallel across the component, and the component under test.
  2. Set the PSU to 0 V. Record V and I.
  3. Increase the voltage in steps (e.g. 0.5 V). Record V and I at each step.
  4. Reverse the connections to the component (or use negative voltage) to take readings for negative V.
  5. Plot I against V. Include the origin (0,0).

Variables

Independent: Voltage V (V)
Dependent: Current I (A)
Control: Same component (don't let filament lamp overheat — turn off briefly between readings), same circuit.

Results

I–V graph

Resistor: straight line through origin. Filament lamp: S-shaped through origin (resistance rises with T). Diode: zero I for negative V; sharp rise above ~0.7 V.
