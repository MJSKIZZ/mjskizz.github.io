Resistance of a wire vs length

Investigate how the resistance of a constantan wire changes with its length. Expect R ∝ L.

Method

  1. Tape a 1 m length of thin constantan wire along a metre ruler.
  2. Connect one end of the wire to the circuit. Use a crocodile clip to contact the wire at a chosen length L.
  3. Connect an ammeter in series and a voltmeter in parallel across the tested length of wire.
  4. Set the PSU to a low voltage (e.g. 3 V) to avoid the wire getting hot.
  5. Record V and I at L = 10, 20, 30, … 100 cm.
  6. R = V / I for each length. Plot R against L.
  7. A straight line through the origin confirms R ∝ L.

Variables

Independent: Length of wire L (cm)
Dependent: Resistance R (Ω)
Control: Same wire material & thickness, same PSU voltage, allow wire to cool between readings.
