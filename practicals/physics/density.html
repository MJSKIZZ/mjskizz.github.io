Density of regular & irregular solids

ρ = m / V. Measure mass on a balance and volume by measuring dimensions (regular) or water displacement (irregular).

Simulation

Mass
— g
Volume
— cm³
Density
— g/cm³

Method — regular object

  1. Place the object on a top-pan balance and record the mass.
  2. Measure the length, width and height with a ruler.
  3. Volume = l × w × h. Density ρ = m / V.

Method — irregular object (displacement)

  1. Place the object on a top-pan balance and record the mass.
  2. Fill a eureka (displacement) can with water to above the spout. Let it drain until water stops.
  3. Place an empty measuring cylinder under the spout.
  4. Carefully lower the object into the can. Water equal to the object's volume pours into the cylinder.
  5. Read the volume of water in the cylinder. This equals the object's volume.

Variables

Independent: The object being measured.
Dependent: Mass, volume and calculated density.
Control: Tare balance, same water temperature, same measuring cylinder.
