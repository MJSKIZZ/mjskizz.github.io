Force and extension of a spring (Hooke's Law)

F = k × e while the spring obeys Hooke's Law (below its limit of proportionality).

Method

  1. Clamp the top of a spring securely. Place a metre ruler vertically next to it.
  2. Measure the natural length of the spring (L₀).
  3. Add a 100 g mass (= 0.98 N) to the spring. Record the new length.
  4. Extension e = L − L₀.
  5. Repeat adding masses in 100 g steps up to 600 g. Record extension each time.
  6. Plot force (y) against extension (x). Gradient = spring constant k.
  7. A straight line through origin shows F ∝ e (Hooke's Law).

Variables

Independent: Force on spring (N)
Dependent: Extension (m)
Control: Same spring, same clamp, same ruler, measure from the same point.
