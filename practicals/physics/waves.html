Waves in a ripple tank

Measure the frequency, wavelength and speed of water ripples. Verify v = f × λ.

Simulation

8
f
— Hz
λ
— cm
v = fλ
— m/s

Method

  1. Set up a ripple tank so that a thin layer of water covers the bottom. Place a lamp above and white paper below.
  2. Connect a straight dipper to a signal generator. The dipper produces plane waves.
  3. Set the frequency on the signal generator (e.g. 10 Hz). f = frequency of dipper.
  4. Count the number of wave fronts that pass a point in 10 s. f = count ÷ 10.
  5. Look at the shadow of the waves on the paper. Use a ruler to measure the distance across 10 wavelengths and divide by 10 to get λ.
  6. Calculate the wave speed: v = f × λ.
  7. Repeat for different frequencies. v should be (approximately) constant in the same depth of water.

Variables

Independent: Frequency of dipper (Hz)
Dependent: Wavelength (cm) — speed = fλ should remain constant
Control: Same depth of water, same dipper, same ruler, same observer.
