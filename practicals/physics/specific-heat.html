Specific heat capacity of a metal block

Use an immersion heater to heat a metal block. ΔE = m × c × Δθ so c = ΔE / (m × Δθ).

Method

  1. Find the mass of the metal block (kg).
  2. Place the immersion heater into the larger hole and a thermometer into the smaller hole. Add a drop of oil to the thermometer hole for good thermal contact.
  3. Wrap the block in insulation (foam or cotton wool).
  4. Connect the heater to a 12 V d.c. PSU via an ammeter. Use a voltmeter across the heater.
  5. Record the starting temperature. Turn on the heater and start a stopwatch.
  6. Record the temperature every 60 s for ~10 min. Record V and I.
  7. Energy supplied ΔE = V × I × t. c = ΔE / (m × Δθ). Plot T against t to find the steepest gradient.

Variables

Independent: Time (s)
Dependent: Temperature (°C)
Control: Same block, same PSU voltage, same insulation, same ambient temperature.
